Career

  • Spinazzola began his career with a former football academy, Virtus Foligno, Siena, and Juventus. He was promoted to the Bianconeri’s senior squad in 2012 but spent the following seven seasons on loan to other Italian clubs. He was first assigned to Empoli, who competed in the Italian league’s second tier, Serie B.
  • He appeared in all seven of Empoli’s matches. After that, his loan period was cut short, and he transferred to Virtus Lanciano, where he played three games before being sent back to his old club, Siena. He made 24 appearances for the Robur, participating in each of their matches that began during his stint.
  • He was signed by Atalanta in August 2014, again on loan from Juventus. He did, however, appear in just two matches. The next season, he joined yet another Italian club, Vicenza, and then Peruga.
  • In July 2016, he returned to the Goddess and became their regular for 48 matches. He was called up to Juventus’ main squad in the summer of 2018 but was sidelined for the first part of the season due to injury. The Foligno-born midfielder made his Juventus debut on January 12, 2019, in a 2-0 Coppa Italia victory against Bologna. On March 12, 2019, he made his Champions League debut, starting in Bianconeri’s memorable 3-0 home triumph against Atletico Madrid.
  • On July 1, 2019, Spinazzola signed a four-year contract with Roma. He has appeared in 51 appearances for the capital club. Spinazzola has been on international duty since 2017, making his debut in the Azzurri’s 2-1 friendly victory over the Netherlands.
