Leonard Fournette | Career

High School

Fournette attended St. Augustine High School, where he excelled in football. 

In his freshman season, the player rushed for over 2,500 yards and 30 touchdowns, becoming the first freshman to receive a scholarship offer from Louisiana State.

Similarly, Leonard finished his sophomore season with 1,900 yards rushing and 27 touchdowns.

As a junior, he had 2,135 rushing yards and 31 touchdowns and was named to the LFCA Class 4A First-Team All-State team.

Leonard was widely regarded as the best running back prospect in his class and was one of the most highly recruited players to come out of Louisiana. 

The player announced his decision to attend Louisiana State University at THE 2014 Under Armour All-America Game (LSU)

College Career

After having an outstanding career at St. Augustine High School, Fournette had near-unprecedented hype heading into college in 2014.

He was named the USA Today High School Football Offensive Player of the Year 2013 and the top-ranked recruit in the 2014 senior class by 247Sports.com, ESPN, and CBS Sports. 

Following his sophomore season at LSU in 2015, he was a consensus All-America selection.

The player set a school single-season record with 1,953 rushing yards and 22 rushing touchdowns.

Professional Career

The Jacksonville Jaguars selected Leonard Fournette fourth overall in 2017. He became the NFL’s youngest player with a rushing touchdown of at least 90 yards.

Fournette also established the Jaguars’ records for the longest rushing play and was the first player to rush for 100 yards in his NFL debut.

He was the first player to score a scrimmage touchdown in each of his first six career games.

He signed a four-year, $27 million contract with the Jaguars in 2017, with an $18 million signing bonus.

Furthermore, Leonard signed a one-year contract with the Tampa Bay Buccaneers on September 6, 2020.

The running back extended his stay with the team with a three-year $21 million contract on march 20, 2022.
