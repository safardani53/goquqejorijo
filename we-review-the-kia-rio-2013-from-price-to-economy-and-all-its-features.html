We review the Kia Rio (2013) from price to economy and all its features

NO firm can say their car is fit for a family until the Dorans have run the rule. Jon, Sheila, Jamie,20, and Christie, 18, and Alfie the labrador take no prisoners ...

KIA have been cashing 9in on the value-for-money market for a number of years.

The style input from Peter Schreyer has meant they have a tasty range to choose from.

But the real feast is that you get a lot of car for your money — and the running bills don't keep draining your wallet.

Kia kept up the green theme earlier this month by announcing a number of initiatives for 2013 that aim to put the environment at the forefront of their business operations.

The focus will not just be on lowering the fuel consumption and tailpipe emissions.

There will be measures to reduce consumption and waste across the entire life-cycle of Kia vehicles.

That is a powerful strategy — especially when you add it to the — famous seven-year warranty for Their cars.

That has been a massive success in their sales strategy.

While the costs appeal to the parents, Schreyer's designs have started attracting young people to the brand.

Take the Rio. It has become a classy hatch that is an ideal first or second buy. Jamie and Christie are more about looks and power. Jamie liked the power the £13,595 1.4 diesel offered.

He was impressed with how easily you can feed the power on, even though the Rio took 13.7 seconds to do 0-60mph and topped out at 107mph. Christie liked the looks — the Rio is longer, lower and better equipped than before.

More Motors

That means plenty of room for passengers and a chance for her to fill the boot with shopping.

For Sheila and me, the 70mpg was a godsend and it is well put together.

That is great news when you have a marauding, and growing, puppy. Alfie is almost full size now and that means he takes up a fair amount of room. But, in his puppy state, he still likes to chew things. Thankfully, the Rio is robust enough to cope.

It is also fun to drive. It is a supermini that likes to be revved, hugs the corners and is very stable even over the potholes.

You could do far worse than putting one on your options list when you are looking for a teenager's first motor.
