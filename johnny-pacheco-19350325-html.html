Johnny Pacheco (Composer) - Age, Birthday, Bio, Facts, Family, Net Worth, Height &amp; More

Juan Azarías Pacheco Knipping, known as Johnny Pacheco, is a Dominican-American musician, composer, composer, conductor, and record producer. He became one of the main representatives of a new dance in the late 1950s called pachanga, a blend of Cuban rhythms and pure dance, which made him internationally famous and played an important role in the development of Latin music. As founder and music director of Fania Records, Pacheco became a leading figure in New York's salsa industry in the 1960s and 1970s. He popularized the use of the term "salsa" and created Fania All. -Stars to showcase the main artists of the genre. Pacheco was nine times nominated for the Grammy Awards and received the Latin Grammy Lifetime Achievement Award from the Latin Recording Institute in 2005.
