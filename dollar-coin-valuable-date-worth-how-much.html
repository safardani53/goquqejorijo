Rare dollar coin sells for $2,861 online - the exact date to look for to find one in your change

YOUR coins could be worth a ton of money if you happen to find a key element, just like with a dollar piece that has sold for more than $2,800 online.

There are a few reasons why coins could be of value including low mintage, errors, as well as grade and condition.

Dollar coins have been struck by the US Mint since 1794.

The current 2022 issue features Sacagawea, a woman who during the Clark Expedition helped explore the Louisiana territory.

But the rarest coins typically feature older dates. 

Here’s what you need to look know about a dollar piece that sold for $2,861 online after collectors put in a total of 54 bids.

The element to look for to find one

The image on the obverse shows an image of Liberty with flowing hair.

And on the reverse, there is an eagle surrounded by a wreath. 

The mint only produced two dates with these designs: 1794 and 1804. 

The dates can be found at the bottom center on the obverse.

The one that sold in the recent eBay listing has the 1804 date, which is actually the higher mintage coin in the series. 

However, it’s still scarce with only 160,295 of these pieces reportedly created.

Also, keep in mind that there are two key varieties with the 1804 date. 

On the wreath, you’ll want to check and see if it has two or three leaves. 

“To determine the variety, simply look at the leaf clusters beneath the eagle's wings -- the clusters will contain either two or three leaves,” coin specialist Ron Guth wrote on the PCGS website. 

“In terms of relative rarity, the 2 Leaves variety is approximately three times as rare as the 3 Leaves variety.”

And luckily for the seller, the dollar coin was the scarcer version. 

How much could it be worth? 

As always, grade and condition play a factor.

But if you happen to find one with two leaves, odds are it will be worth thousands.

According to 1794 two-leaf sales posted by Professional Coin Grading Services, the value could range from as low as $1,947 to $105,750 in circulated grades. 

The one in the eBay listing was certified by Numismatic Guaranty Company (NGC) in “F details”. 

The circulated grade contains “moderate wear on many elements with heavy wear on high points,” according to NGC. 

What other dollar coins are valuable?

There are of course other coins you’ll want to look out for when sorting through your spare change. 

Namely, the 1893-S (San Francisco) Morgan Dollar, with one recently selling for nearly $13,000 online. 

The piece, which only has a mintage of 100,000, is worth $4,511 in average condition and could be worth $662,418 in an MS-65 grade, according to USA Coin Book. 

Another key date is 1795, which is engraved on the draped bust dollar. 

Along with the grade, the value of the 1795 date could also depend on the variety, which includes the centered and off-center bust types.

Also, one 1795-dollar coin recently sold for $3,061.

Another dollar coin to look out for is the 1872 Carson City (CC) piece.

The mintage is even lower, with just over 3,000 of those coins produced.

The piece could be worth as much as $109,542 in an MS-63 grade, or $3,317 on average.

To see if your spare change is worth anything, you can check eBay by searching the full name, selecting the "sold" listing, and then toggling the search to "highest value”.
