  • Joey Mantia began rollerblading as a self-described “rink rat,” often begging his parents to take him to the local indoor skating rink. He initially went to the Olympic Training Center in Colorado Springs, Colorado, and then two years later to Salt Lake City, Utah, to train with the national team.  Mantia placed fourth in the 5000 m, eleventh in the 1000 m and eighth in the 1500 m at the 2011 US speed skating championships.
  • Mantia debuted on the World Cup competition circuit in early 2013. He rediscovered success less than a year later, winning gold in the 1500 m at the Berlin World Cup in December 2013, knocking off his colleague Shani Davis, a two-time Olympic silver medalist in the 1500 m, and Russia’s Denis Yuskov, the defending world champion.
