High School & Collegiate Career

Tari Eason spent his first two years at Garfield High School but saw minimal playtime during that period.

He decided to switch to Federal Way High School for his junior year and played alongside top recruit Jaden McDaniels.

However, he returned to Garfield for his senior year & led his team to a 3A state title. He finished that season averaging 23 points and 8 rebounds per game.

Eason committed to play college basketball for Cincinnati, where he averaged 7.3 points and 5.9 rebounds per game as a freshman.

He then transferred to LSU for his sophomore year and had a remarkable season, averaging 16.9 points and 6.6 rebounds per game.

On March 25, 2022, he declared for the 2022 NBA draft and decided to forgo his remaining college eligibility.

NBA Journey Of Tari Eason

In the 2022 NBA draft, the Houston Rockets selected Eason as their 17th overall pick.

Tari Eason made his NBA debut on October 19, 2022, playing against the Atlanta Hawks and scoring 8 points.

He recorded his career’s first double-double on December 3, 2022, against the Golden State Warriors scoring 13 points and grabbing 10 rebounds.

On January 21, 2023, he started in the main rotation for the first time against the Minnesota Timberwolves and scored 14 points.

Eason recorded his career high of 21 points on March 20, 2023, against the Warriors in a 108-121 loss.
