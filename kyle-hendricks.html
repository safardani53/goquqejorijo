Early Career- Minor Leagues and the Beginning

Kyle Hendricks is a star pitcher for the Chicago Cubs, and we cannot deny his contribution to the team. But the achievement and results did not happen overnight.

In fact, Hendricks has been at it since his high school days. During the 2008 MLB draft, the Los Angeles Angels picked him in the 39th round.

Instead of signing with them, young Kyle chose to attend Dartmouth College and played for the college team under head coach Bob Whalen.

Moving to his college year, Kyle played collegiate summer baseball with the Brewster Whitecaps of the Cape Cod Baseball League.

Then in the 2011 MLB draft, the Texas Rangers picked Hendricks in the eighth round. This time though, he signed with the team and began his professional career as the Spokane Indians of the Class A-Short Season Northwest League.

The following year, Hendricks was exchanged to the Chicago Cubs for Ryan Dempster.

It would be fair to say, Kyle really shined with the Cubs. Right after the trade, the young Kyle started with the 2013 season with the Class AA Southern League’s Tennessee Smokies. They even named Hendricks the minor league pitcher of the year.

Similarly, on July 10, 2014, Kyle made his Major League Baseball(MLB)debut with the Chicago Cubs against the Cincinnati Red at the Great American Ballpark.

Twelve days later, he earned his first win in front of a home crowd against the San Diego Padres. With that, Kyle ended his rookie season with a 7-2 win-loss record and a 2.46 ERA.

Following his impressive rookie season, Hendricks started the 2015 season as a Cubs’ starting rotation member. Then on May 21, Hendricks threw his first career complete-game shutout against the Padres, striking out seven and facing three batters over the minimum.

That year Kyle’s no decisions were the most among MLB starting pitchers, where he also recorded 8-7 with 180 innings and an ERA of 3.95.

Likewise, Hendricks opened game two of the 2015 National League Division Series and round three in the 2015 National League Championship Series against the New York Mets.

Chicago Cubs- 2016 to Present

With each passing year, Kyle just kept on improving, and 2016 was no different as well.

He finished the first half of 2016 with a solid 7-6 record with a rotation leading 2.55 ERA. In September, he recorded 13-7 and earned a run average of 2.09 in 159 innings pitched. Without a doubt, Hendricks was named NL Pitcher of the August. 

Overall, Kyle finished the season with a record of 16-8 in 190 innings pitched and an ERA of 2.13.

He was also the first Cubs to lead the National League in the state since 1945 and the first to show the majors since 1938. Similarly, with Kyle as the starting pitcher, the Chicago Cubs won the 2016 World Series after a victory over the Cleveland Indians.

Expected to start the 2017 season with a bang, Kyle was placed on the disabled list in June, following his middle finger tendon inflammation in his right hand.

After a month of rest, Hendricks returned to the pitch and posted an improved 2.19 ERA in 78 innings pitched. Despite the injury, Kyle ended the season on a good note.
