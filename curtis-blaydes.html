Curtis Blaydes | Professional Career

Early Career

Curtis began his early career as a wrestler after joining De La Salle Institute. There he won the state title in his senior year with an undefeated 44-0 record.

His four-year overall record includes 95-18 with 121 takedowns. The young boy even played football at De La Salle Institute.

After joining Northern Illinois University as a full wrestling scholar, he scored a record of 19-2 as a redshirt freshman.

Later on, after transferring to Harper College, Curtis won NJCAA National Championship as a redshirt sophomore.

Thereafter, he chose to pursue his career as a professional wrestler rather than a study. So he began fighting in amateur mixed martial arts with an overall score record of 8-0.

Before signing to UFC (Ultimate Fighting Championship) in 2014, he won an overall score of 5-0 via TOK(Technical Knockout).

Professional Career

Razor began his professional career at UFC Fight Night 86, where he made his debut against Francis Ngannou on April 10, 2016. However, he lost the match at the end of the second round via TOK.

The same year on October 1, the young MMA fighter faced Cody East at UFC Fight Night 96. He won his first UFC Performance of the Night bonus after winning the match at the second round via TOK.

At the UFC Fight Night 104, Razor faced Adam Milstead, winning the fight via TOK, where Milstead suffered from a knee injury.

But later on, the result was changed to No Contest after Blaydes tested positive for marijuana. This incident took place on February 4, 2017.

On July 8, 2017, at UFC 213, the Chicago resident faced Daniel Omielanczuk, winning by unanimous decision.

Similarly, the same year on November 4 at UFC 217, Blaydes faced the fight against Alexey Oleynik, winning the fight due to the doctor’s advice.

During the fight with Oleynik, Razor got an illegal kick from the opponent fighter. Later on, after the doctor followed up and advised, the injury was not much deep.

With a unanimous decision on February 11, 2018, Curtis won the fight against Mark Hunt at UFC 221.

The same year on June 9, after facing Alistair Overeem at UFC 225, the American resident won the fight. The win earned him be Performance of the Night bonus.
