The 8 Best Portable Blenders of 2024

No matter whether you want to make a smoothie, a sauce, or a soup, a blender is a truly invaluable appliance. The only drawback? Just like a stand mixer, they’re big and bulky, oftentimes occupying prime real estate on your kitchen counter or in your cabinets. They can also get expensive, with some models costing upwards of hundreds of dollars; even if you’re using one daily, that’s a serious investment. And that’s where portable blenders come up huge.

As the name suggests, these scaled-down versions of their OG counterparts take up far less space and ring in at a fraction of the cost. While they generally aren’t as powerful as full-size versions, the latest models still pack a powerful pureeing punch.

“Portable blenders are an amazing invention, a quick and convenient way to get a lot of nutrients into your diet through smoothies and soups,” says Haylie Pomroy, health strategist and the New York Times best-selling author of The Fast Metabolism Diet. “Newer ones are more lightweight than ever before and can serve as both a blender and a drinking vessel. These are great for home if you have limited counter space, to keep at the office, or to travel with,” she adds. And again, while they generally are less powerful than the big boys, if your blending needs are simple and you’re not typically blending for a crowd, or if you travel a lot, a personal blender may be a good option for you, Pomroy points out.

To help you narrow down your choices, here are the best portable blenders currently on the market.

Things to Consider Before Buying a Portable Blender

Weight

Particularly if you’re looking for an option for travel, it’s a good idea to choose one that’s on the lighter side, suggests Pomroy. Take the BlendJet 2 Portable Blender for example, coming in at a mere 1.52 pounds — an incredibly transportable and easy-to-store option. However, this is less important if you’re going to be keeping it out on your kitchen counter.

Power Source

Cordless portable blender options that can be charged via USB or USB-C are great for travel or at the office, when you can charge them from the port on your computer, Pomroy notes. Our best cordless blender, the Cuisinart EvolutionX Cordless Compact Blender, charges with a USB cord so you can power it up anywhere instead of needing to be close to an outlet (so you can skip the smoothie shop and make your own by the pool if you want to).

Maintenance

Make sure all of the components of a personal blender can easily be taken apart for cleaning purposes, Smart suggests. Bonus points if the cups, lids, and blades can all be tossed in the dishwasher. However, be sure to read the fine print, because not all of our picks are dishwasher safe. The last thing you want is melted plastic or ruined blades because you tossed out those precious instructions.

Motor Power

Not all portable blenders are going to be able to blend up ice, notes Petrucci, or chop up bigger pieces of frozen fruit or leafy greens. Think the Ninja Fit Compact Personal Blender, loaded with 700 watts of power and supreme ice-chopping capabilities. Consider exactly what you like to whip up in your blender and choose accordingly.

Price

While you can find quality portable blenders at many different prices, it’s important to take into consideration how much you’re willing to spend for the features you’re looking for. More budget-friendly options like the Hamilton Beach Personal Blender are lighter on the wallet but have very simplistic features, whereas our best overall, the NutriBullet Single-Serve Blender, which is programmable, has different-sized cups and lids, and comes with a 1200-watt motor, is also our priciest pick. 

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Are portable blenders worth it?

    If you’re regularly making smoothies, soups, or sauces, then yes. These can be a much more affordable alternative to traditional blenders or food processors, and will save space in your kitchen, too. If you don’t find yourself needing a blender too often, a more budget-friendly option may be your best starting point. But if you are on the protein shake or green smoothie grind, it’s likely worth it to invest in a high-quality machine that may be priced on the higher end to ensure quality and longevity.

  • What is the point of a portable blender?

    Portable blenders are an easier, more convenient way to be able to create things such as daily smoothies. They’re also ideal for anyone who wants to maintain their eating routine while traveling or needs to be able to whip up quick things at the office. This means that it can either be taken on the go, still connected to its stand, or taken in the cup as a makeshift glass to fit in with your daily life.
