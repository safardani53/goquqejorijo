Who is Dippy the Diplodocus, when is the dinosaur's UK tour and what's replaced him at the Natural H

DIPPY The Diplodocus is the UK's most famous dinosaur and has had a starring role at London's National History Museum since 1979.

But now the dinosaur's star status is set to spread across the country as he hits the road for a tour of Britain which will see him go on public display outside London for the first time.

What is Dippy the Diplodocus?

The replica dinosaur was cast from original fossil bones discovered in the US in 1898.

He came to the Natural History Museum in 1905 and was moved to the central hall in 1979.

The 70ft plaster-cast sauropod replica has dominated the Natural History Museum's vast Hintze hall ever since.

He became an instantly recognisable symbol of the London museum, standing just inside the main visitors' entrance.

Dippy is a Diplodocus, a herbivore with a long neck that it would have used to reach high and low vegetation and water.

It is one of the longest dinosaurs and lived during the late Jurassic Period, about 150 million to 155 million years ago.

Why has Dippy left the Natural History Museum?

The museum is having a front-of-house makeover so the decision was made to retire Dippy and replace him with a blue whale.

He's spent his time out of the spotlight getting spruced up for a tour of the UK which will last until late 2020.

This will involve re-making some parts and giving him a new, modular support structure, or armature, to facilitate frequent packing and unpacking.

The move went viral on social media with the launch of a Twitter campaign under the hashtag SaveDippy. More than 32,000 people signed a petition calling on the museum to change its mind.

What has replaced Dippy?

Dippy has been replaced by the giant skeleton of a blue whale, called Hope.

It has been suspended in a diving pose from the ceiling of the Hintze Hall.

The near-4.5-tonne whale specimen is more than 100 years old and has been given the name as a "symbol of humanity's power to shape a sustainable future".

The Natural History Museum acquired the skeleton after it opened in 1881 when it beached in Wexford in Ireland.

Curators paid Â£250 for the carcass and nearly every bone has been carefully prepared for the skeleton to take centre stage at the museum's entrance.

Will Dippy go back to the Natural History Museum?

He will return to the museum, with plans to re-cast the dinosaur in bronze and place him in the gardens that surround the building.

Museum director Sir Michael Dixon said: "We wanted Dippy to visit unusual locations so he can draw in people that may not traditionally visit a museum.

"Making iconic items accessible to as many people as possible is at the heart of what museums give to the nation, so we have ensured that Dippy will still be free to view at all tour venues."

Where is the first stop on Dippy's tour?

The dinosaur will kick off his national tour at Dorset County Museum, which has a gallery dedicated to Britain's fossil-rich Jurassic Coast.

He appeared 130 miles away at the museum on February 9 and will spend at least four to six months at the museum before moving on to his next home.

Dr Jon Murden, director of the Dorset County Museum, said he was "excited" at the prospect of Dippy's visit.

He added: "As the birthplace of palaeontology, there is nowhere in the UK more appropriate for Dippy to start the tour than Dorset, and we're thrilled to have been chosen as the first host venue."


Where can you see the famous Diplodocus on tour?

DIPPY will visit Scotland, Wales, Northern Ireland and five regions across England.

The skeleton will be on show at the following venues:

  • Dorset County Museum, February - May 2018
  • Birmingham Museum and Art Gallery, May - September 2018
  • Ulster Museum, September 2018 - January 2019
  • Kelvingrove Art Gallery and Museum, Glasgow, January - May 2019
  • Great North Museum: Hancock, Newcastle upon Tyne, May - October 2019
  • National Assembly for Wales, October 2019 - January 2020
  • Number One Riverside, Rochdale, February - June 2020
  • Norwich Cathedral, July - October 2020

Where's next on Dippy's tour?

In chronological order, Dippy will then travel to Birmingham Museum; Ulster Museum; Kelvingrove Art Gallery and Museum, Glasgow; Great North Museum, Newcastle; the National Assembly of Wales; Number One Riverside, Rochdale; and Norwich Cathedral.

The venues were chosen from an application list of 90 candidates when plans for the tour were announced last year.
