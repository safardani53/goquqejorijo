'Raging' cop 'assaulted colleague in pub toilet' after rumours the officer was sleeping with his wif

A 'raging' detective sergeant repeatedly punched a colleague in a pub toilet over rumours the officer was sleeping with his wife, a court heard.

DS Alistair Stilgoe, 46, threw a flurry of blows at DS Peter Blackburn after grabbing him round the neck in the gents of the Boot and Flogger in Southwark.

The pair were among colleagues necking Champagne at the pub to celebrate a fellow officer getting the all clear to suspected testicular cancer on 16 June last year.

The prosecutor explained that both DS Stilgoe and DS Blackburn were married to high ranking police officers.

Stilgoe’s wife DI Amanda Mawhinney, from whom he was estranged, was Mr Blackburn's supervisor at Twickenham police station.

He was said to have 'spread rumours' at Acton police station that the pair - Blackburn and Mawhinney - had been sleeping together on Sundays.

"The background to this case is that both the complainant, Peter Blackburn, and the defendant, Alistair Stilgoe, are both serving police officers.

"At the time of this alleged offence, Mr Blackburn was detective sergeant at Twickenham police station and Mr Stilgoe was a detective sergeant at Acton police station.

"The defendant's wife was the complainant's supervisor at Twickenham police station and at the time she was the acting detective inspector.

"At the time of this alleged offence, the defendant and his wife were estranged and there were not in fact living together.

"On 15 April 2016, the complainant's wife Kate Blackburn, who is also a police officer, a detective sergeant in the domestic violence unit at Wimbledon police station, received information that Mr Stilgoe had been telling people at Acton police station that her husband was having an affair with his wife.

"He was spreading a rumour and Mrs Blackburn was obviously concerned about this. She rang up the defendant to find out what was going on."

Stilgoe initially denied spreading the rumour, but then 'his stance change somewhat and he questioned where DS Blackburn was every Sunday, suggesting he was with his estranged wife.

"He also said he had overheard a conversation which seemed to him to be too familiar," said the prosecutor.

"The complainant (DS Blackburn) told his wife that he was not having a relationship with Ms Mawhinney.

"The Crown's position is that there was no relationship between Ms Mawhinney and Mr Blackburn."

Jurors heard there was no further incident until June 16 last year when Stilgoe bumped into DS Blackburn at the Boot and Flogger.

Stilgoe was drinking with three other officers, DS Mark Davies, DC Shaun Bolt and PC Robert Adam to celebrate DS Davies being 'given the all clear with regard to suspected testicular cancer.

The policemen were on their third bottle of champagne when Mr Blackburn, along with his friends Dom Beary and barrister Sam Brown, arrived at the wine bar at around 6:50pm.

Jurors heard DS Davies approached DS Blackburn and told him he was with Stilgoe, who was 'f**king raging'.

DS Davies then warned DS Blackburn: 'You should leave, he thinks you are f**king his wife.'

DS Blackburn made his way to the toilet when he was approached by Stilgoe who was 'described as looking angry and started shouting some drunken tirade about his wife,' said the prosecutor.

"The complainant told him there was nothing going...but as he tried to leave through the exit, Mr Stilgoe grabbed him around the back of his head and neck, dragged him to the back of the toilet."

Mr Blackburn 'felt a number of punches to the left side of his head,' before he was able to make his way outside.

Stilgoe again tried to attack him, only to be restrained by DC Bolt, jurors heard.

He suffered 'superficial injuries' to his face and part of one of his wisdom teeth came out, Mr Moll said.

When Stilgoe was arrested, he dismissed the alleged assault as 'handbags at dawn'.

He claimed that Mr Blackburn approached him and said, 'You f**king called my wife.'

In interview Stilgoe maintained he acted in self-defence when Mr Blackburn punched him to his right eye.

He added that his wife had not admitted or denied the alleged affair and that Mr Blackburn had 'had two affairs with people's wives at Ealing.'

Stilgoe, of High Wycombe, Bucks denies one count of assault occasioning actual bodily harm.

DS Peter Blackburn said that he was promoted to detective sergeant in June 2015 under acting detective inspector Amanda Mawhinney.

He said: "I had spoken to her once on the telephone when she called me up to welcome me to the borough. I was aware she was married to Mr Stilgoe.

"My understanding was that they had been broken up for a long period of time shortly after I moved to Twickenham and she started a new relationship."

Mr Moll asked: "Was that with you?"

Mr Blackburn replied: "No, not at all."

He added: "Months before the assault my wife challenged me on it [the rumour.] The allegation was that the weekends when I was at working, I wasn't actually at work, I was with Amanda Mawhinney. Certain things like I had been to the cinema."

Mr Blackburn said he had not even met Stilgoe at this point, but heard no more about the matter until the incident on 16 June.

He said that he met DS Davies as he arrived at the Boot and Flogger, adding that he was 'very drunk and the first thing he was was he apologised for being very drunk.'

They spoke briefly and parted, before DS Davies 'came back to me and said he was with 'Ally' Stilgoe, saying he was raging.

'He said: 'I'm with Ally Stilgoe, he's f**king raging. He thinks you're f**king his wife, you should leave.''

In cross-examination, Heather Oliver suggested Mr Backburn had 'attempted to physically intimidate [Stilgoe].'

Mr Blackburn replied: "You are incorrect."

Ms Oliver suggested he had shouted at Stilgoe: "You called my f**king wife."

Mr Blackburn replied: 'I said, "you have called my wife, you've caused me chaos.”'

He added: "The whole situation was upsetting but, on that night, I didn't know Mr Stilgoe. I just wanted to get out of there. It's ridiculous, we are police officers.

"I've got no interest in fighting people in pubs. It's not what I do."

The trial continues.
