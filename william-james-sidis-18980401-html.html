William James Sidis is an American child prodigy with exceptional math and language skills. He is best known for his 1920 book The Animate and the Inanimate, in which he speculates on the origin of life in a thermodynamic context. Sidis was brought up in a special way by his father, psychiatrist Boris Sidis, who wanted his son to be gifted. Sidis first becomes famous for his wisdom, then his eccentricity and his withdrawal from public life. In the end, he avoided math altogether, writing about other subjects under certain pseudonyms. He entered Harvard at the age of 11 and as an adult it was confirmed that he had an extremely high IQ and could speak around 25 languages ​​and dialects. Some of these claims have not been verified, but many of his contemporaries, including Norbert Wiener, Daniel Frost Comstock and William James, supported the claim that he is extremely intelligent.
