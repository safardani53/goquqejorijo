Who is Brent Barry? Early Life, Parents & Siblings

Brent Robert Barry shortly, Brent Barry, is an American basketball player, executive, and broadcaster. He was born in Hempstead, New York, to parents Rick Barry and Pam Hale.

Additionally, his father is a former NBA and ABA Basketball player and was named one of the 50 Greatest Players in history by the NBA in 1996.

Brent’s parents divorced when he was very young, and his father got married to stepmother Lynn Barry. Adding to that, Lynn was also a distinguished basketball player in college.

Apart from his parents, Brent has four siblings: 3 brothers named Jon Barry, Scooter Barry, Drew Barry: and a sister named Shannon Barry. Also, he has a half-brother named Canyon Barry.

Moreover, Jon is also a former basketball player and current television analyst for ABC and ESPN. Same as that, his other two brothers Scooter and Drew, are also former basketball players. 

Not to mention, Brent’s half-brother Canyon is also a talented basketball player for the Iowa Wolves of the NBA G League.

Brent Barry | Early Career

Brent started his NBA career after getting selected by the Denver Nuggets in the first round 15th pick of the 1995 NBA draft.

Moreover, Barry first played for the three seasons and was considered a good passer with averaging more than five assists per game.

Likewise, he was also an intense three-point shooter and ranked 13th all-time in career three-point field goals.

In 1996 Barry won the Slam Dunk Contest in the NBA All-Star Weekend, being the first caucasian player to win the competition.

Similarly, he was also on the San Antonio Spurs championship teams in 2005 and 2007.

Brent Barry | Professional Career

Barry was traded immediately to the Los Angeles Clippers after being drafted by the Denver Nuggets. In his rookie season, Brent made 123 3-pointers breaking the rookie record. 

Moreover, Brent and the Clippers tried a playoff run in the second season, where he got 11.7 ppg, which was his highest in the postseason.

Likewise, during the postseason, Brent played in 3 games with the clippers. 

Likewise, on January 25, 1999, Barry signed a 6-year $27 million contract with the Chicago Bulls.

But sadly, he had to deal with an ankle injury due to which he played only 37 of the 50 games that season, starting 30 of those games, averaging 11.1 ppg.

Subsequently, after failing to fill the Bull’s void, he was traded for Hersey Hawkins and James Cotton from Seattle on August 12, 1999.

After that, Brent began his Seattle career with fellow Oregon State alumnus Gary Payton.

There he played the point position as a starter and filled in when needed as a small forward.

Similarly, he spent four seasons with the Seattle SuperSonics and played ten postseason games, the most in his career thus far.

In 2004, Brent signed as a free agent with San Antonio Spurs and spent most of the season as a backup.

Likewise, he helped them beat the Nuggets in the series four games to one and earned his first championship ring defeating the Detroit Pistons in the 2005 NBA finals.

Continuing with his form, in June 2007, he won his second NBA championship ring after the Spurs defeated the Cleveland Cavaliers.

In January 2008, Brent tore his right calf muscle and could not play for a month due to his injuries.

Nevertheless, on February 20, 2008, Barry, alongside Francisco Elson, was traded by the San Antonio Spurs back to the Seattle SuperSonics in exchange for Kurt Thomas. 

After a 30-day waiting period, Brent re-signed with San Antonio on March 24, 2008, for one year.

Coming off of injury, he did not play much in the first two rounds of the 2008 NBA playoffs.

However, San Antonio provided him with the most playoff experience with 71 games, the most of his career. 

Further, in 2008 Barry signed a 2-year contract with the Houston Rockets, becoming the third member of the family to join the franchise.

Later in 2009, he was cut off from the Rockets, which led to his career end.

Brent Barry | Post-playing career

After ending his playing career, Barry started making regular appearances on NBA TV’s The Starters in his segment The Bone Zone in 2013. 

Moreover, he served as a fill-in analyst for the NBA on TNT coverage and play-by-play announcer for their Players Only broadcast starting in 2016.

Likewise, Brent also partnered with Ian Eagle for TNT’s coverage of the 2018 NBA playoffs.

Not to mention, in 2018, Barry joined the San Antonio Spurs front office as their vice president of basketball operations.

Is Brent Barry married? Personal life and Kids

Brent was married to his long-time girlfriend, Erin Barry, with whom he had been together from the time they were in high school.

They first met while Erin was a student at an all-girls Catholic school in San Francisco, and Brent was in the all-boys school across the street.

Furthermore, after dating for a couple of years, the two tied the knot in 1998. From their relationship, Brent and Erin have two sons together.
