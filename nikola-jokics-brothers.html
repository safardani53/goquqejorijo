A Brief Bio of Nikola Jokić

Early Life

Born on February 19, 1995, Nikola plays center for the Denver Nuggets of the National Basketball Association (NBA).

Nikola grew up in a small two-bedroom apartment with his parents, grandmother, and two brothers.

Nikola spent the majority of his childhood shooting hoops. His two elder brothers were interested in basketball and played it too, which sparked his interest in the sport as a child.

His love for basketball was also the result of his big brothers’ influence.

At an early age, Nikola exhibited some exceptional basketball skills and was quite popular in the city. Thus, he was known as the “basketball player.”

He joined Mega Bemax, a Bel-grade-based basketball team after he turned 16. Nikola played for the Junior team in his first season playing for Mega Bemax.

Soon, the club recognized his talent and promoted him to the next season’s senior national team. In 2005, he won the Top Prospect award and achieved the title of the most valuable player in the league of the Adriatic League.

Awards And Accolades

Fast forward to 2014, Nikola was drafted as the 41st overall pick in the 2014 NBA draft by the Denver Nuggets. Just a season in the game, he proved himself a worthy pick and an excellent asset for the team.

Similarly, in 2016, he was entitled to the honorary title of the NBA All-Rookie Team. On top of that, he ranked 3rd in the NBA Rookie of the Year Award.

The following year, in 2017, he was the runner-up of the NBA Most Improved Player Award and the Assist of the Year award voting.

Moreover, Nikola also holds the record for the fastest triple-double in the history of the NBA league.

In 2021, he earned the MVP of the Year award, which made Shaq O’Neal more than happy.

“You’re the first big man to win an MVP in a long time. I thank you, and I appreciate you very much,” said O’Neal to the Serbian giant.

Jokic became the NBA’s lowest-drafted player and the first player in Denver Nuggets franchise history to be crowned MVP.

Jokic won the NBA Most Valuable Player Award for the second year on May 12, 2022, defeating finalists Joel Embiid and Giannis Antetokounmpo.

When he won his second consecutive MVP, he rode to the stables in Serbia on a two-wheeled cart drawn by his horse.

Moreover, after advancing to the playoffs as the 1 seed of the Western Conference, the Nuggets, led by Jokic and Murray, won the 2023 NBA championship.

In the 2023 NBA Finals, Jokic averaged 30.2 points, 14 rebounds, 7.2 assists, and 1.4 steals, earning him the NBA Finals MVP.
