Brad Stockton Wife: Is Texas Ranger Married Or Dating Anyone?

The former Texas Rangers minor league player Brad Stockton married last year in November. Brad tied the knot with Ashley Stockton on November 16, 2022.

Ashley is native to Clanton, Alabama, and after her wedding to Brad, the couple began living in Woodstock, Georgia. Mrs. Stockton graduated from the University of Alabama with a degree in communicative disorders in 2008. 

Who Is Ashley Stockton?

The University of Alabama graduate, Ashley Stockton, worked at Alfa Insurance as a Customer Service Representative right after her getting out of college. 

After working for the insurance company for a couple of years, Ashley joined the multinational fast-food restaurant franchise Subway. At Subway, Ashley was the general manager of five locations.

She was responsible for generating weekly employee schedules within the budgeted labor goals, maintaining and executing updated cash inventory and POS Control System. 

Ashley returned to Alfa in 2017 after working for six years at Subway. In 2020, Ashley joined another Insurance company, Allstate, in Huntsville, Alabama. She worked for a year and a half as an Insurance agent. 

Early in 2022, Ashley joined Colten Mortgage as a Mortgage Loan Originator but only worked there for four months. Earlier this year, on May 4, 2023, Ashley established the company Axelle Candle, starting her journey as a CEO. 

Ashely is available on Facebook and has shared several family photos, including pictures from her wedding with Brad. She has also shared a few pictures of the candles produced by her company, Axelle Candle. 

Brad Stockton Career After Baseball

Stockton played for years of collegiate baseball at Georgia Institute of Technology. He started 183 games at Georgia, with an average of .323, and scored 24 home runs.

After completing his studies at Georgia in 2001, Stockton was drafted by the Rangers in the 22nd round. He was assigned to Class A Pulaski, where he tied for league-lead with 18 doubles and earned All-Star honors. 

He was later promoted to Class A Savannah and began the second season with them. In 2002, Brad played 90 games for Savannah and had a batting average of .231, an OBP of .343, and a slugging percentage of .313. 

But the numbers weren’t good enough for Brad to keep him floating in Texas. In 2003, Brad was released by Texas after Spring Training.

Soon after leaving the baseball world behind, Brad joined Pfizer as a sales representative. He worked there for three years before joining Alcon Laboratories as a Specialty Sales Representative.    

Currently, Brad works at QGneda as a Senior Account Executive in 2022 and previously worked in the same position for Healthy.io. He also worked as Sales Executive for Tract Manager from 2019-2022. 

Brad is available on Facebook but hasn’t shared many posts on his social media handle.
