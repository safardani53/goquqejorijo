  • Contestants don’t have a glamorous life on set

    During the two months that The Bachelor/Bachelorette films, contestants do not have access to their phones or the internet.

    Contestants must make arrangements to take care of their finances while they are gone, as they have no access to the outside world.

    It has also been claimed that contestants are responsible for doing their own hair, makeup, and selecting their wardrobes.

    The show only provides a stylist for the premiere episode and for the final two contestants for the finale.

  • Host Jesse Palmer found love

    Jesse married Emely Fardo on July 5, 2020, but the pair didn't share the news until a year later.

    Fardo, 37, is a Brazilian-born model and photographer.

    She and Jesse met at a New York City boxing class in 2017, before he popped the question in 2019.

    Jesse proposed in France with an oval 3.5-carat diamond ring lined with other diamonds on each side.

  • About Bachelorette host Jesse Palmer

    Jesse Palmer is a TV personality and former contestant on The Bachelor.

    The 44-year-old was part of season five of the show, which aired back in 2004.

    Palmer is also a former NFL quarterback and a sports commentator on ESPN.

    He previously served as a special contributor on Good Morning America and across ABC News.

  • Making history

    Charity is the fourth Black Bachelorette in the franchise’s history, which is something she has fully recognized the importance of.

    “When I was told that I was going to be the next Bachelorette, that was one of the first things that I immediately thought of,” she told Entertainment Tonight.

    “To step into this role as the fourth woman of color… it’s huge,” Charity continued.

    “I recognize that, although it is my journey, it’s so much bigger than me, because I’m carrying along a whole community of people who are looking up [to me] and [I’m] representing that as well.”

  • How much do contestants get paid?

    The contestants do not get paid to be on the show.

    Despite contestants competing for free, many of them do receive social media sponsorships after their time on the show.

    However, Reality Steve reported that the Bachelorette makes $100,000 for his time on the show.

  • 'It's not just physical'

    In an op-ed for The Hollywood Reporter, Bachelorette Andi Dorfman spoke about the fantasy suites, saying:

    “Sure, most of the time there’s intimacy involved. But it’s not just physical, it’s emotional as well."

    Adding: “The experience is a kind of unveiling of the fantasy, in an ironic way. It’s the first time you get to spend time with this person that you’re dating without producers.”

  • Fantasy suites explained

    The remaining three contestants on the hit reality show are given the opportunity to spend the night with the Bachelor or Bachelorette in the fantasy suites episode in a luxurious private bedroom.

    There are no cameras in the fantasy suites, but the microphones usually stay on.

    Usually, contestants do not really divulge the details of what happens in the fantasy suites, but they are allowed to have sexual relationships if they desire.

  • What to know about winner Dotun Olubeko

    Born on August 22, 1992, Dotun Olubeko is an integrative medicine specialist and reality TV contestant from Brooklyn, New York.

    Dotun owns DPT Fitness; an online health and wellness service.

    He’s a graduate of both California State University, Chico, and the University of Western States; where he studied nutritional sciences.

    Although he resides in Brooklyn, he and his family are originally from Nigeria.

    “We came here with nothing, but my parents took on whatever job they could,” he said about his parents and childhood during his series introduction.

    “They’ve done really well to give me a life that I’m happy to live so it’s been a really humbling experience.”


  • Keeping the ring

    Winning couples who become engaged by the end of The Bachelorette can keep the engagement ring for free… as long as they don’t break up.

    Couples who call it quits within two years of becoming engaged on the show must return the ring.

  • How to join The Bachelor, continued

    The second way applicants can apply is via a casting call.

    Fans can find more information on when and where casting calls are held on BachelorNation.com.

    In-person applicants are asked to fill out a more detailed form with questions about why their past relationships ended, why they want to find love on television, and what they find most attractive, physically and personality-wise, in a future spouse.

    Former contestants have also revealed that some casting is done through social media and casting directors have reached out on Instagram.

    It’s important to have an open account where producers can access potential candidates the platform.

  • How to join The Bachelor

    There are two ways to apply to be on The Bachelor or Bachelorette.

    Firstly, there is an online application that can be completed at BachelorNation.com.

    The questions on the form for each show are basic, asking for name, age, height, what your occupation is, and if you have been married before or have children.

    There is also a question about why you want to be on The Bachelor or Bachelorette. It asks applicants to submit their social media handles and whether or not they know someone else who they think would be good for the show.
