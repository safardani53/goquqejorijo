You'll never believe how much these celebs get paid to turn on the Christmas lights in your town

DON'T be fooled into thinking Christmas light switch on events are just for washed up celebrities to try TO raise their profile.

They are in fact incredible money spinners, with stars earning thousands of pounds for their ability to flick a switch and spend an hour wishing townsfolk a Merry Christmas.

PETER ANDRE

One of the highest reported earners is singer Peter Andre, with The Mirror previously revealing the 45-year-old was paid a whopping £20k to turn on the Christmas lights in Perth, Scotland in 2014.

For the fee, Peter performed a string of hits including Mysterious Girl.

NADINE COYLE

Meanwhile The Daily Telegraph reported that former Girls Aloud singer Nadine Coyle and Scouting for Girls were paid £14k each to switch on St Helens lights in 2015. The same council reportedly paid boyband 5ive £5,000 for flicking the switch in 2017.

OLLY MURS

Renfrewshire Council reportedly paid Olly Murs £15k back in 2010 and a similar fee to Stacey Solomon the following year.

The same council was still splashing the cash last year, with The Mirror reporting rock band Ash received £12k to do the honours.

Moment Oxford Street Christmas lights were turned on in 2017

MARK WRIGHT

MailOnline reported in 2013 that Mark Wright was paid £7,000 plus flights and travel for an hour's work to turn on Perth's Christmas lights.

But now he's made his name across the pond as a correspondent on Extra, his fee is bound to have increased if he's invited to star in another Christmas light switch on.

BEN HAENOW

X Factor winner Ben Haenow received £8,500 to appear at Carlisle's festivities in 2016, two years after winning the show.

A year later his fee had dropped slightly to £6,500 for the Blackburn with Darwen Council switch on.

BRIAN MCFADDEN

Another pop star cashing in is former Westlife singer Brian McFadden, who was reportedly paid £2,500 by Denbighshire Council in Wales to perform six songs and switch on the lights in 2013.

Meanwhile Chorley Council paid The Voice winner Kevin Simm £2,000 in 2016.

DICK AND DOM

The Telegraph revealed back in 2010 that kids TV presenters Dick and Dom charged £5,800 to turn on Solihull's Christmas lights.

SOAP STARS

Former EastEnders actress Cheryl Fergison secured £1,200 from Kettering Council in Northamptonshire in 2016, while Coronation Street's Jennie McAlpine reported earned £3,000 for a Christmas light switch on in Stockton back in 2010.
