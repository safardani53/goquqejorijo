Boyfriend murdered lover by smashing her over the head with a sledgehammer before sleeping next to h

A BRUTAL killer bludgeoned his lover to death with a sledgehammer then slept for the night next to her body, a court heard.

James D'Arcy, 50, confessed to cops the next day: "I pummelled her head into a pizza. She will never f*** with anyone again."

His hairdresser girlfriend Hayley Dean, 38, was found in bed with her head under a pillow, and with a cigarette still in her lips and a lighter in her hand.

She had suffered a sustained a brutal attack, with skull fractures, multiple cuts to her scalp and forehead and injuries to her right hand.

The day after the murder D'Arcy went to the home of a friend, Robert Wilson, and told him: "I need to talk to you seriously, I have done something bad. I have killed my missus."

He added: "I wrapped a duvet over her head before I hit her with a sledgehammer. She is dead, I have slept with her all night.

"I definitely done it, there's blood all up the wall."

Mr Wilson took D'Arcy, who said he wanted to take pills and die next to Hayley, to a police station.

He told officers: "I killed my girlfriend, I smashed her head in.

"I made her head into a pizza. I pummelled her head into a pizza. She will never f*** with anyone again."

D'Arcy continued: "I have just shoved my bird's head in. I am not really f****** happy about it but she deserved it."

He also said: "I have just been done for murder, can I have some hot food and drink please."

He was found guilty of murder today after a trial at Winchester crown court.

Kerry Maylin, prosecuting, told the court the defendant had been living with the victim for about three months in Bournemouth, Dorset.

She said: "The relationship between them can at best be described as turbulent.

"Neighbours described loud music, offensive language and excessive drinking which escalated when Mr D'Arcy moved in."

She said a neighbour heard Hayley shouting "Get out of my flat" on September 15 last year before hearing a "slow and steady deep thud" about 15 times with intervals of 10-15 seconds.

D’Arcy carried out a series of seemingly normal tasks including getting on a bus, withdrawing cash, buying food from McDonald's and topping up his mobile phone in the 24 hours before he told anyone about Hayley’s death, the jury heard.

Detective Inspector Neil Phillips said after today's verdict: "James D'Arcy carried out a sustained and brutal attack on Hayley Dean as she lay helpless in her bed.

"I hope the verdict given by the jury today will bring some closure for Hayley's family after what has been a traumatic and devastating time for them all."

After the killing, Hayley's family said in a statement released through Dorset Police: "Hayley was a very bubbly and funny girl who was adored by everyone she met.

"She had lots of friends and enjoyed spending time with them. She always had a smile on her face and was full of life. She is going to be very sadly missed.

"She enjoyed fashion, make-up and hair and loved to make herself look nice. She completed a hairdressing course and it was her dream to open her own salon one day."

D'Arcy will be sentenced tomorrow.
