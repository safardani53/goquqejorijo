Jeopardy! viewers call out contestants after all three flunk final clue that was obvious to Taylor S

JEOPARDY! fans have called out all three contestants on Tuesday's episode after they failed to answer a question correctly.

Many Taylor Swift fans thought the answer was obvious.

Host Mayim Bialik introduced the category, which was "Literary Groups."

"Windemere, Thirlmere, & Grasmere are three of the sites that helped give a 19th century literary group this name," the clue read.

They started with returning champion, Diandra D'Alessio, who answered "What are the Algonquin Round Table?"

Nathan Dennis answered: "Who are the Meadwomen?"

Ilhana Redzovic answered the same as Diandra and everyone was wrong.

Mayim let everyone know that the corrected response was "Who are the Lake Poets?"

"Made up of Wordsworth, Coleridge, and Southee."

SWIFTIES KNOW!

Taylor Swift has a bonus track on her eighth studio album, folklore, called The Lakes.

The song features the lines: "Take me to the lakes where all the poets went to die / I don’t belong, and my beloved, neither do you. These Windermere Peaks look like the perfect place to cry."

It is about going to The Lakes district with her "muse" and leaving the rest of the world behind.

The lakes features both the site and the poets mentioned in the Final Jeopardy! question.

Swifties were stumped that the three contestants didn't know the answer and took to Twitter to call them out.
