Jason Belmonte | Early Life & Family

Jason Belmonte was born to his parents, Aldo Belmonte and Marisa Belmonte, on July 29, 1983, in Orange, New South Wales. Moreover, Jason’s father was one of the star players in his city.

In fact, he even opened the Orange Ten Pin Bowl when Belmonte was just a toddler.

Other than that, we believe that the 36-year-old is the single child of his parents because we could not find any details on that matter.

Moving on, Jason started playing with bowling balls even before he could walk.

To illustrate, in an interview conducted in 2009, Belmonte said,

“The only problem for me though was as an 19 month old baby boy lifitng a nine to ten pound (4-4.5 kg) bowling ball….it was a little hard, so like all babies, I pushed the ball with two hands.”

The quote mentioned above tells you everything you need to know about the Australian player. Even at such a young age, nothing could hold the young kid down. And, fast forward 34 years later, he is currently the best player in the whole world.

Jason Belmonte | Bowling Career

When we take a look at Belmonte’s Career, it is nothing short of extraordinary. As a matter of fact, it is littered with record-breaking accomplishments and championships.

Throughout his illustrious 14-year professional career, Jason has won 24 PBA Tour titles, which includes thirteen majors.

Consequently, the Australian is the only player in PBA’s history to win 13 major titles, putting him in a league of his own.

Added to that, Belmonte has also won three world finals, European Bowling Tour, World Tenpin Masters, and World Bowling Tour (WBT).

And, if that wasn’t enough, the 36-year-old has 25 career 300 games (highest score possible) in PBA Tour events.

Well, by now, you must be thinking that all his achievements and trophies have been listed.

But wait! There’s more because Jason is only the second man in history to win the Super Slam. To explain, Super Slam is achieved when a bowler wins all five PBA Major titles.

Thus, for his outstanding contributions to the bowling world, Bowlers Journal magazine named Jason as the Male Player of the Decade (2010-2019).

Likewise, we fully agree with the decision because players like Belmonte come once in a generation.

Besides that, he secured the second position in the 2007 World Ranking Masters. The same year, he participated in the AMF World Cup, where he won the country rankings for Australia.

Similarly, as part of Team Australia, he won the trios bronze medal in the 2014 WBA Men’s World Championship.
