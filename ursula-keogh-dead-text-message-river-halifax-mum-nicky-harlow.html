Heartbreaking last text of schoolgirl, 11, to her novelist mum before she was found dead in Halifax

A TRAGIC schoolgirl sent a heartbreaking last text to her mum saying "I love you but I'm so sorry" before she was found dead, an inquest heard.

Ursula Keogh, 11, sent the message to her novelist mum Nicky Harlow hours before her body was found in a river in Halifax last Monday.

Bradford Coroner's Court today heard how the schoolgirl had a history of self harming.

Her body was found in the River Calder after she vanished from a bridge just yards from a radio station where her mother Nicky, 52, presents a weekly show.

Mrs Harlow, a mum-of-two, has written a series of books, including Amelia and the Virgin and The Glass Stairs.  She also lectures in creative writing for the Open University.

Coroner’s officer Bernard Tate told the court Nicola reported her daughter missing to police after receiving the heartbreaking text message.

Ursula’s mobile phone was found on the bridge above where she was discovered.

Assistant Coroner David Urpeth said: “This is an utterly tragic case involving the death of a young girl.

“The circumstances surrounding the death have been submitted to police for investigation.”

The inquest was adjourned and is expected to resume on May 22.

Following her death, Ursula's school, The Lightcliffe Academy released a statement paying tribute.

It said: "We look to support students and the whole academy community with this heartbreaking loss.

"Our thoughts and prayers are with Ursula’s family and friends and at this very difficult time."

Nicola's manager at Phoenix FM, Howard Priestly, added: "The impact has been massive on the community.

"It has been such a big shock.

"Ursula's sister Abbie came on work experience here a while ago.

"I don't know much about Nicola's past but they are a very nice family."

A bunch of flowers had been laid outside the family home.

Tributes to Ursula flooded social media after news of her death broke.

One wrote: “Absolutely heart breaking, rest in peace young soul. May whatever come next treat you with beauty and grace. May your mum and dad find peace in the beautiful memories you gave them. God bless."

Eric Robinson said: "There are no words. Such tragic news thoughts go to her family and loved ones."

Donna Vasey added: "I read the earlier headlines, just hoped she had popped to a friend’s house. Sad so sad."

Tina Jackson posted: "Yet another sad day so early in the year of a young child losing their life. My heart goes out to her family."

A West Yorkshire Police spokesman said: "Enquiries will remain ongoing to determine a cause of the death, however, at this stage, it is not believed there are any suspicious circumstances."
