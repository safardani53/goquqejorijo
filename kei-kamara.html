Kei Kamara | Early Life 

Kamara (fully named Kei Ansu Kamara) was born on 1 September 1984 under Virgo’s sun sign to his single parent, Fatima Kamara. He was born in the second-largest city in Sierra Leone, Kenema. 

Like an action movie, Kamara has his early childhood narration. During his childhood, his mother won a 1990 immigration lottery and left for Los Angeles to help support the family. 

She sent him to live with his aunt and cousins in Sierra Leone, where the civil war between its army and anti-government rebels commenced.

He lived in a large family where his elder brothers kept him safe when rebels and government soldiers alternately lived with them for weeks.

Kamara saw everything as a child that shouldn’t be known or seen. Additionally, he knew terrors, fears, and death all too well for a kid.

He witnessed the forced recruiting of children into the war and the death that followed those who refused. 

“I lived through it my whole childhood. It was very, very scary. I tried to understand these things I heard about the Taliban and Al-Qaeda, and I’m like, ‘That’s not what I was taught as a Muslim.”
-Kei Kamara

Reunion With Mother & Life Onward

Kamara fled Sierra Leone with his family and moved to the Gambia when he was 14, and he waited two years before finally reuniting with his mother.

However, he first moved to Baltimore, where his uncle resided, and by age 16, he moved to Los Angeles as part of a refugee program.

Nearly a decade later, Kamara united with his mother and, thus, started attending California State University, Dominguez Hills as an American citizen. 

As disclosed by Kamara, the children there mocked him due to his stuttering voice and accent. However, as he joined soccer and starred in it, nobody ever made fun of him again. 

During his college career, Kamara successfully bagged third-team All-American. Apart from it, he also played two seasons for the Orange County Blue Star in the USL Premier Development League.

Kei Kamara | Club Career 

Kamara started his club career right after college.

The foremost club he worked with is Columbus Crew which selected him in the first round, ninth overall at the 2006 MLS SuperDraft.

He spent two seasons with that club, where he had 36 gameplays. 

San Jose Earthquakes

In 2008, Kamara was traded to the San Jose Earthquakes for Brian Carroll.

However, his time with the club was short-lived, and he only had 12 appearances filled with two goals. 

Houston Dynamo

He was again traded to the Houston Dynamo IN 2008, for whom his career was also quite short.

Sporting Kansas City

Following it, Kamara played for Sporting Kansas City after Dynamo traded him in 2009. This moment marked his turning point.

Apparently, he played quite a few years with the team and even featured himself as one of the most imposing wingers in the league.

Later, he was then loaned to the Norwich City of the English Premier League.

Norwich City

Kamara’s debut match for the club only came after the visa grant that made him a legal player.

To elaborate, his debut match was an 86th-minute substitute in a 0–0 draw with Fulham. 

Likewise, he was a 58th-minute substitute for his second match, followed by backing up team-mate Wes Hoolahan.

It was also the same moment when he became a new idol for soccer, as many songs were composed for him. 

Following it, Kamara declines the contract for his permanent transfer to the team. 

Middlesbrough

After his contract with Norwich ended, he played for a while with Sporting Kansas. Then, he moved to Middlesbrough of the Championship on a two-year contract for £900,000.

He first commenced as the 63rd-minute substitute and made his foremost start in the game at Nottingham Forest. At the same time, he also earned his first goal for the team.

After a few arrays of gameplay, they parted ways with mutual consent. 

Columbus Crew

Kei returned to his former team, Columbus Crew, in 2014 and was added to the roster in 2015.

Additionally, he first played in the playoffs, thus, leading the team to the Eastern Conference and finishing second. 

He starred as the joint top-scorer of the league alongside Toronto FC’s Sebastian Giovinco.

Apparently, he also claimed the 2015 MLS Landon Donovan MVP Award and the 2015 Advocare MLS WORKS Humanitarian of the Year Award. 

Kamara posted wins over Montreal Impact and New York Red Bulls. Despite the injury, he featured in the 2015 MLS Cup Final against the Portland Timbers. 

New England Revolution

On 12 May 2016, Kei Kamara was traded to the New England Revolution, where he had the first score over the New York Cosmos.

Alongside his team, he also gained his foremost MLS hat trick on 2 September 2017.

Vancouver Whitecaps FC

Likewise, Kamara moved to the Vancouver Whitecaps FC on 10 December 2017 and posted his foremost goal against Montreal Impact. 

Colorado Rapids

Similarly, after entering Colorado Rapids on 11 December 2018, where he earned the second hat trick of his professional career.

Minnesota United

After coming into Minnesota United on 18 September 2020, he became the first MLS player to score for eight teams. 

HIFK Fotboll

The professional soccer player signed with HIFK Fotboll in Finland on 30 July 2021.

CF Montreal

Kamara returned to North America in February 2022, joining CF Montreal on a year deal with an option for 2023.

He debuted for his new club on 23 February against Santos Laguna of Liga MX in the 2022 CONCACAF Champions League match.

According to FoxSports, during the match on 13 September, Kei scored two goals with one assist on four shots victory against Chicago Fire.

He led CF Montreal into a matchup with the New England Revolution fresh from a two-goal outing against the Chicago Fire.

Kei Kamara scored a goal and drew two fouls during the 9 October draw 1-1 match against Inter Miami.

Kei Kamara | Controversy

When you get into the spotlight, there is nothing that goes away. Even after years and years, the news is bound to circulate, and at times, things get controversial.

It gets out of hand no matter what you meant or have done. 

2016 Penalty Kick

On 7 May 2016, Kei Kamara got involved in a heated argument with Federico Higuain of Columbus Crew SC over who would take a penalty kick just after halftime.

Back then, Columbus Crew SC was leading the game by 3-1, and a goal again from the penalty would give them the insurance for the game’s victory. 

However, both athletes argued about the penalty, which ended after Higuain got the chance.

Altogether, the match ended as a 4-4 draw, and later in the talk with the press, Kamara openly discussed his opinion calling Higuain out. 

“I haven’t really had to depend on Pipa [Higuain] at all. How long have I been here? How many goals have I scored? And how many have come from his assists? I don’t depend on him. How many times have me and [Higuain] connected?”
-Kei Kamara

Altogether, the incident ended after head coach Gregg Berhalter chose to suspend Kei Kamara.

Thus, the internal conflicts between players bought huge trouble for the team. Therefore, within just five days of suspension, Kamara was traded to New England Revolution.
