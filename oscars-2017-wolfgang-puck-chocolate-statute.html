Chef Wolfgang Puck on His Chocolate Oscars

Only a few of the attendees at this year’s Academy Awards will walk away with one of its coveted golden statutes — but everyone will walk away with another desired prize: an edible Oscar, courtesy of Wolfgang Puck.

That’s right: As the celebrity chef explained on the red carpet ahead of the show, “Everybody’s gonna get an Oscar from us.” He was referring to a sweet-treat miniature version of the award he created and passed out at the show.

“If you’re hungry, you can eat it,” he told PEOPLE Deputy Editor JD Heyman. Indeed, the little “statutes” are made of 24-karat gold (just like the real thing) and chocolate.

Puck — the mastermind of the menu for the annual Governor’s Ball after the Oscars and overseer of dozens of whimsical dishes — was joined on the red carpet Sunday by his youngest son, Oliver, who revealed how he helped with this year’s spread.

Along with one of his brothers and “a little” help from Puck, Oliver cooked a themed chicken pot pie. The Oscars touch? The pie’s crust had the silhouette of the golden man baked into it.
