Benidorm more popular than ever as outrageous fancy dress festival lures 25,000 Brits after quiet su

BENIDORM has smashed its hotel occupancy record thanks to a 'bad taste' fancy dress festival attended by more than 25,000 Brits.

Tens of thousands of fun-loving holidaymakers descended on the popular hotspot last week to take part in Costa Blanca's legendary fancy dress day.

Now hotel owners have revealed they were 88 per cent full during the first two weeks of November - the best stats since records began.

Many believe it is the sometimes-shocking dressing up festival which has helped transform Beni-doom into Beni-boom.

The bizarre annual parade takes place the day after the town's official fiesta ends, and is billed as Europe's largest fancy dress party.

The upbeat tourism news comes a few months after the resort was forecasting dire times ahead saying the number of Brit visitors was in freefall.

The hotel association Hosbec says the trend has changed over the last month and the behaviour of the British market has "normalised."

The unexpected bankruptcy of the airline Monarch had resulted in an accumulated loss of 420,000 overnight stays by Brits since January.

"Despite the growth of Spanish tourists, as a whole the international market remains the majority in Benidorm (54.6 per cent)," it said.

"In addition to Spanish and British, the only two markets with a notable share are Belgium and Holland."

Its president, Toni Mayor said the 88.1 per cent occupancy rate was going to be amongst the best in Spain but warned against complacency.

"Nobody should think these figures are the result of chance or circumstantial circumstances," he said.
"The sector has worked hard and invested a lot to achieve these results."He praised hotels which had spent millions on refurbishments and said others should follow the example.Mayor also urged Benidorm to work towards being a "fun, modern and safe" destination and to foster the "spirit" shown during the fancy dress party.The event is regarded as the biggest of its kind in Europe and rounded off the traditional Benidorm fiesta celebrations. Brit expats and tourists parade in costumes at Benidorm’s annual ‘bad taste’ fancy dress contest

Thousands join in the outrageous street party with bars, hotels and entertainment venues along “the strip” offering paellas, music and entertainment.

Hosbec says air capacity is getting back to normal, with 20,000 more seats being offered compared to the previous year "showing that demand responds when there is capacity to fly."

he forecast of hotel occupancy in Benidorm for the second half of November is 77.3 per cent.
